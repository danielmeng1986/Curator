# 01 · Vision

> *A Personal Digital Asset Intelligence Platform*

---

# Vision

Curator is a long-term platform for organizing, understanding, and preserving personal digital assets.

Its purpose is not simply to store files, but to transform a growing collection of digital content into structured knowledge that remains understandable and valuable over time.

Curator combines human expertise with artificial intelligence to build a trustworthy, maintainable, and extensible knowledge system.

---

# Why Curator Exists

Modern digital life continuously generates new assets:

- photographs
- videos
- scanned documents
- books
- music
- screenshots
- AI-generated content
- project files
- personal archives

As collections grow over many years, traditional folder structures become increasingly difficult to maintain.

Searching becomes harder.

Metadata becomes inconsistent.

Knowledge becomes fragmented.

Eventually, valuable information is lost—not because the files disappear, but because they can no longer be understood.

Curator exists to solve this problem.

---

# Beyond File Management

Curator is **not** intended to become another file browser.

It is **not** designed to replace the operating system.

It is **not** a media player.

It is **not** simply a photo management application.

Instead, Curator focuses on understanding digital assets through structured metadata, reproducible workflows, and AI-assisted analysis.

The objective is to create knowledge rather than merely organize files.

---

# Human-Centered Intelligence

Artificial intelligence plays an important role in Curator, but it is never the owner of the collection.

AI assists by:

- recognizing patterns
- generating metadata
- detecting relationships
- proposing improvements
- reducing repetitive work

Human users remain responsible for final decisions.

Curator is designed around collaboration between humans and AI rather than automation alone.

---

# Long-Term Thinking

Curator is expected to evolve over many years.

Technologies will change.

Programming languages will change.

AI models will change.

Storage technologies will change.

The architecture should remain understandable despite those changes.

Every major design decision should increase:

- maintainability
- reproducibility
- readability
- extensibility

instead of optimizing only for today's implementation.

---

# Knowledge Over Files

Files are only one representation of information.

Curator focuses on preserving the knowledge surrounding those files.

Examples include:

- relationships between assets
- people appearing in images
- locations
- events
- studios
- albums
- semantic meaning
- user annotations
- AI-generated understanding

Over time, this knowledge becomes more valuable than the original folder hierarchy.

---

# Design Philosophy

Curator follows several fundamental principles.

## Preserve Originals

Original assets should remain untouched.

Every transformation should be traceable.

---

## Build Structured Knowledge

Information should be stored in structured form rather than inferred repeatedly.

The database represents accumulated knowledge.

---

## Prefer Explicit Decisions

Important operations should be visible, reviewable, and reversible.

Curator values transparency over hidden automation.

---

## AI Should Assist, Not Replace

Artificial intelligence should enhance human productivity rather than remove human control.

Suggestions are welcome.

Final decisions belong to the user.

---

## Documentation Is Part of the Product

Architecture, design decisions, and project philosophy are first-class assets.

Documentation is considered part of the system itself.

---

# Future Vision

The long-term objective of Curator is to become a unified platform for personal knowledge preservation.

Future capabilities may include:

- semantic search
- visual similarity
- face recognition
- object recognition
- natural language querying
- automatic organization
- timeline reconstruction
- knowledge graphs
- recommendation systems

These features should emerge naturally from the architecture rather than being isolated additions.

---

# Mission Statement

Curator transforms personal digital assets into long-term knowledge.

It combines structured data, reproducible workflows, and artificial intelligence to help people preserve, understand, and continuously enrich their digital collections.

The project values clarity over complexity, knowledge over file organization, and long-term maintainability over short-term convenience.

---

> Curator is not built merely to manage today's archive.

> It is built to preserve the knowledge contained within a lifetime of digital assets.
