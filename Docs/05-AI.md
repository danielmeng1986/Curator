# 05 · AI

> Artificial Intelligence Architecture

---

# Overview

Artificial Intelligence is a core capability of Curator.

However, AI is **not** the center of the system.

Curator is designed around structured knowledge.

AI exists to enrich that knowledge.

The architecture therefore separates:

- data
- knowledge
- intelligence

so that AI models may evolve without requiring architectural redesign.

---

# AI Philosophy

Curator follows a human-centered AI philosophy.

Artificial Intelligence should:

- reduce repetitive work
- generate suggestions
- discover hidden relationships
- enrich metadata
- improve searchability

Artificial Intelligence should **not**:

- replace human judgment
- modify original assets directly
- introduce irreversible changes
- become the source of truth

The database remains the source of structured knowledge.

Humans remain responsible for important decisions.

---

# AI Architecture

The conceptual AI architecture consists of several independent services.

```
                +--------------------+
                | Curator Database   |
                +---------+----------+
                          |
          +---------------+---------------+
          |               |               |
          v               v               v
+----------------+ +----------------+ +----------------+
| Vision AI      | | Language AI    | | Rule Engine    |
+----------------+ +----------------+ +----------------+
          |               |               |
          +---------------+---------------+
                          |
                          v
                +--------------------+
                | Knowledge Layer    |
                +--------------------+
```

Each AI service contributes knowledge independently.

No single model owns the system.

AI may assist in the following areas.

## Recognition

Examples:

- face recognition
- object recognition
- scene understanding
- logo recognition
- text recognition (OCR)

Recognition transforms raw media into structured observations.

---

## Understanding

Examples:

- image captioning
- semantic description
- event understanding
- visual reasoning

Understanding provides context rather than labels.

---

## Classification

Examples:

- studio classification
- album categorization
- content grouping
- quality estimation

Classification organizes information.

---

## Relationship Discovery

Examples:

- duplicate detection
- similarity search
- related albums
- recurring models
- recurring locations

Relationships transform isolated assets into connected knowledge.

---

## Recommendation

Examples:

- rename suggestions
- album titles
- metadata completion
- tag suggestions
- workflow optimization

Recommendations support human decision making.

---

## Knowledge Generation

AI continuously enriches the Knowledge Layer.

Examples include:

- semantic tags
- embeddings
- descriptions
- confidence scores
- inferred relationships

Generated knowledge remains distinguishable from human-authored information.

---

# AI Service Types

Curator intentionally separates different kinds of AI.

## Vision AI

Responsible for visual understanding.

Examples:

- face recognition
- object detection
- image captioning
- scene understanding

---

## Language AI

Responsible for reasoning and language generation.

Examples:

- documentation
- metadata generation
- album naming
- search interpretation
- workflow assistance

---

## Embedding Services

Responsible for semantic representation.

Examples:

- image embeddings
- text embeddings
- cross-modal embeddings

Embeddings enable similarity search and semantic retrieval.

---

## Rule Engine

Not all intelligence requires machine learning.

Rule-based processing remains important.

Examples:

- naming conventions
- validation rules
- duplicate naming detection
- consistency checking

Rules provide deterministic behavior.

---

# Human and AI Collaboration

Curator follows a collaborative workflow.

```
AI Analysis

↓

Suggestion

↓

Human Review

↓

Approval

↓

Knowledge Update
```

AI proposes.

Humans curate.

Knowledge grows over time.

---

# Knowledge Lifecycle

AI-generated information follows its own lifecycle.

```
Observation

↓

Prediction

↓

Confidence

↓

Human Review

↓

Accepted Knowledge
```

Not every prediction becomes knowledge.

Knowledge requires verification.

---

# Confidence

Every AI result should include confidence information whenever possible.

Examples:

- face match confidence
- similarity score
- recognition probability

Confidence helps users evaluate AI suggestions.

Confidence is not certainty.

---

# Explainability

Whenever practical, AI should explain its conclusions.

Examples:

Instead of:

"Duplicate."

Prefer:

"92% visual similarity and identical capture date."

Transparency improves trust.

---

# Replaceable Models

Curator avoids coupling architecture to specific AI models.

Possible implementations may include:

- local models
- cloud APIs
- open-source models
- future multimodal systems

Changing a model should not require redesigning the Knowledge Layer.

---

# Local First

Whenever practical, Curator prefers local AI execution.

Benefits include:

- privacy
- reproducibility
- offline capability
- user ownership

Cloud services may be integrated when appropriate, but they should remain optional.

---

# AI Safety

AI should never:

- modify original assets without approval
- overwrite verified knowledge automatically
- hide uncertainty
- remove human oversight

Safety is considered an architectural principle rather than an implementation detail.

---

# Future Directions

Potential future AI capabilities include:

- natural language search
- conversational archive exploration
- timeline reconstruction
- automatic collection building
- knowledge graph generation
- multimodal reasoning
- memory-assisted workflows
- personalized recommendations

These capabilities should extend the architecture without changing its principles.

---

# Summary

Artificial Intelligence is an extensible layer built on top of Curator's structured knowledge.

AI exists to enrich understanding rather than replace human expertise.

The architecture intentionally separates intelligence from data, allowing AI technology to evolve while preserving long-term maintainability.

---

> AI generates possibilities.

> Humans create knowledge.

> Curator preserves both.
